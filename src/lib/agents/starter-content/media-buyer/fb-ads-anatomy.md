# Meta ad anatomy - the four-part structure

Every paid social ad on Meta that ships in the top 10 percent of an
account has the same four parts. Skip one and the ad caps at mediocre.

## 1. Hook image (or hook three seconds, on video)

The single asset that earns the scroll-stop.

For static: the image has to be readable as a thumbnail. Test it on a
phone at arm's length; if you cannot tell the subject in 0.5 seconds
the asset is broken. Patterns that work:

- Native UI (a screenshot of a result, a chat thread, a metrics
  dashboard).
- Loud color contrast against the platform's default greys.
- A face mid-emotion, not a stock smile.

For video: the first three seconds carry 80 percent of the watch
decision. Patterns that hold:

- Action mid-motion, no establishing shots.
- A bold on-screen claim in the first frame, with the speaker reacting
  to it.
- Native pattern interrupt (subway, kitchen counter, car) instead of
  studio polish.

## 2. Hook line (primary text, line 1)

The first 90 characters before "...see more" is the only line that
matters on mobile. Every other line is a bonus.

Three patterns that hold up across accounts:

- **Specific result + timeline.** "$3,847 from a one-page funnel in 11
  days."
- **Pattern interrupt.** "Most consultants are pricing themselves
  $40k under what the market would pay."
- **Direct callout.** "Agency owners over $50k MRR: read this before
  you raise rates."

Never start with the brand name on a cold ad. Brand-first hooks lose
50-70 percent of the click-through against pain-first hooks on the
same audience.

## 3. Value-stack body

The middle 100-200 words. Three jobs:

1. Prove the claim from the hook is real (one line of mechanism, one
   line of result, one line of named proof).
2. Pre-handle the top objection ("but does this work for B2B" /
   "but I have tried funnels before"). One sentence.
3. Make the offer concrete. What is the reader getting, in plain
   English. No "comprehensive solution" copy.

Keep the body in short paragraphs. Two sentences max per paragraph on
mobile.

## 4. CTA

One verb. One destination. Match the verb to the awareness stage.

- Cold traffic: read, watch, download (low friction).
- Warm traffic: book, buy, apply (offer commit).
- Retargeting: finish, complete, claim (closing the open loop).

Always test the CTA button text against the body text. Mismatch
between body promise and button promise is the most common reason for
high CTR / low landing-page conversion.

## The Meta ad audit

Before any ad goes into a fresh ad set, run the four-part check:

- [ ] Hook asset readable in 0.5 seconds on mobile
- [ ] Hook line under 90 characters, pain or specifics, not brand
- [ ] Body has mechanism + result + named proof
- [ ] CTA verb matches awareness stage
- [ ] Offer in body matches landing page H1 word for word

A failure at any step blocks publishing. The cost of a broken ad is
not the spend, it is the audience burn - Meta will not show the next
ad as cleanly to a fatigued audience.
