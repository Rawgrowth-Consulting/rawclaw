# Hook tear-downs

Five hook patterns that have shipped on paid traffic in the last 12
months. For each: the pattern, an example, why it works, and the
failure mode.

## 1. The contrarian fact

> "The best-paid copywriters in 2025 do not write copy. They write
> systems."

**Why it works.** Reverses the reader's frame on the first beat.
Contrarian only works when the reader's existing belief is strong;
weak beliefs do not snap back hard enough to hold attention.

**Failure mode.** Too contrarian and the reader rejects the writer
before reading the body. The fact has to be defensible inside the
first 200 words or the ad reads as clickbait.

## 2. The specific number plus timeline

> "$11,400 from one cold email in nine days. No follow-up sequence,
> no calendar dump."

**Why it works.** Specificity carries credibility. "Nine days" lands
harder than "fast"; "$11,400" lands harder than "thousands".
Readers default to skepticism; precise numbers slow the skim.

**Failure mode.** If the number cannot be backed up in the body the
ad burns trust faster than a generic hook. Never lead with a number
the case study cannot prove.

## 3. The named avatar callout

> "Agency owners over $50k MRR who are tired of feast-or-famine months:
> this one is for you."

**Why it works.** Self-selects the right reader and tells the wrong
reader to scroll. Better targeting than any algorithm. Works
particularly well on platforms where audience targeting is loose
(LinkedIn, X) and weaker where targeting is tight (Meta).

**Failure mode.** Too narrow ("agency owners with three employees in
Austin") and the audience is too small to scale. Too broad
("entrepreneurs") and it does not select.

## 4. The before-state mirror

> "If you have ever closed a $20k deal then woken up at 3am wondering
> when the next one is coming, this is the rest of the story."

**Why it works.** Names a private experience the reader has had but
rarely names. Recognition triggers attention faster than persuasion.

**Failure mode.** Requires deep avatar research. If the experience
named is not authentic to the reader the hook reads as manipulative.
Pull these from sales call transcripts, never from imagination.

## 5. The mechanism reveal

> "Here is the one structural shift that takes a coaching offer from
> $2k retainers to $15k engagements without changing the deliverable."

**Why it works.** Promises a how, not a what. The reader who has
already tried the obvious moves is hungry for a structural answer.
Works best for sophisticated avatars (Schwartz stage 4+).

**Failure mode.** If the body does not actually deliver the
mechanism the ad reads as bait-and-switch. The mechanism must be
real, specific, and explainable in under 60 seconds.

## How to use this file

When asked to write a hook:

1. Pick three patterns from the five above.
2. Write one hook in each pattern. Stay under 12 words on the first
   line.
3. Rank them by which pattern matches the reader's awareness stage
   (Schwartz). Cold readers respond to specifics and avatar callouts;
   warm readers respond to mechanisms and contrarian facts.
4. Ship the top one. Keep the other two as variants for split tests.

Never ship a single hook without two backups. Hooks decay fast. The
ones that work this quarter will not work next.
