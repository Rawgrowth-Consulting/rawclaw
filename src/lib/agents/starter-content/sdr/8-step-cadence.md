# The 8-step outbound cadence

Two-week cadence. Six touches, three channels. Designed for high-trust
B2B outbound where the prospect knows the category but not the brand.

## The cadence

| Day | Channel | Action | Goal |
|-----|---------|--------|------|
| 1   | LinkedIn | View profile, no connection | Surface in their notifications |
| 1   | Email | First touch (75 words max) | Earn the read |
| 3   | Email | Reply on the same thread | Bump for opens |
| 5   | LinkedIn | Connection request + 200-char note | Get on their feed |
| 7   | Voicemail + Email | "Sent you a voicemail" email | Multi-channel pulse |
| 11  | Email | Referral pivot (ask for the right person) | Open the org |
| 15  | Email | Break-up email | Clean exit |

Sequence ends day 15. Prospects who do not respond go cold for 90
days, then re-enter on a fresh trigger.

## Touch 1 - email (day 1)

Structure:

1. **Personal line** (first 12 words). Reference one specific public
   signal. Funding round, job change, podcast appearance, product
   launch. Generic flattery does not count.
2. **Insight line** (one sentence). Tie the signal to a concrete
   problem the prospect is likely facing right now.
3. **Offer** (one sentence). What you do, in plain words. No company
   name in this line.
4. **CTA** (one yes/no question). Never a calendar dump.

Example:

> Saw the Series A announcement last week, congrats - the seven-person
> sales team is the next bottleneck.
>
> Most teams at your stage hit 60 percent of pipeline coverage by
> Q3 hiring alone, then plateau.
>
> We help post-A teams add 40 percent more qualified pipeline through
> outbound without adding headcount.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call next Tuesday or Wednesday?

Body length: 75 words ceiling. 60 is better.

## Touch 2 - email (day 3)

Reply on the same thread. One sentence. No new pitch.

> Bumping this in case it slid past the inbox.

That is the entire email. Single-sentence bumps lift open rates 30-40
percent against multi-paragraph follow-ups.

## Touch 3 - LinkedIn connection (day 5)

Connection request with a 200-char note. The note is not a pitch; it
is a recognition.

> Hi Sam - saw the Series A announcement last week. Following the build
> at [Co]. Would value the connection.

Never pitch in the connection note. Save the pitch for after the
prospect accepts.

## Touch 4 - voicemail + email (day 7)

Leave a 30-second voicemail. Same structure as touch 1: personal,
insight, offer, CTA.

Send a follow-up email titled "Voicemail just landed" with one
sentence: "Just left a voicemail with a 15-minute ask. If easier,
just reply to this email."

Multi-channel pulses lift response rates by 60-80 percent against
single-channel follow-ups.

## Touch 5 - referral pivot (day 11)

When the prospect has not responded in 11 days, the assumption is the
target is wrong, not the message. Pivot:

> Sam - it is possible I am pinging the wrong person. If pipeline
> coverage is owned by someone else on the team, would it make sense
> to introduce me?

Referral pivots open 20-30 percent of stalled cadences, often onto a
warmer contact than the original target.

## Touch 6 - break-up (day 15)

Clean exit. Lifts response rates by 15-25 percent against simply
ending the cadence.

> Sam - I have not heard back so I will close the loop on my end.
> Happy to circle back when the pipeline question is on the desk again.
> Best of luck with the Series A build.

Never use guilt or sarcasm in a break-up. The goal is permission to
re-open in 90 days, not a clever last word.

## What this file is for

When asked to write a sequence, return all six touches at once.
Label each with day, channel, goal, and word count. Do not deliver a
cadence as a single email plus "follow up later"; that is not a
cadence, that is a guess.
